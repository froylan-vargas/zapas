# Senior Azure AI Engineering Residency

## Vision

Become a Senior Azure AI Engineer by building, evolving, defending, and operating a single production-quality application while preparing for the AI-200 certification as a natural milestone rather than the primary objective.

---

# Philosophy

This residency is based on five principles:

1. Learn through one evolving product (Zapas).
2. Every technology must solve a real problem.
3. Architecture decisions must be intentional and documented.
4. Understanding and communication are more valuable than memorization.
5. Every week leaves the codebase in a better state than the previous one.

---

# Residency Phases

## Phase 1 – Cloud Foundations
Weeks 1–2

Goal:
- Build a cloud-ready ASP.NET Core application.
- Understand Azure fundamentals through implementation.
- Establish engineering practices and deployment pipelines.

Deliverables:
- Initial Azure deployment
- Architecture diagram
- ADR process
- CI/CD baseline

---

## Phase 2 – Cloud-Native Engineering
Weeks 3–5

Goal:
- Design reliable distributed systems.
- Introduce messaging, identity, security and observability.

Deliverables:
- Event-driven workflows
- Managed Identity
- Key Vault
- Monitoring and telemetry
- Production diagnostics

---

## Phase 3 – AI Cloud Development
Weeks 6–7

Goal:
- Integrate AI responsibly.
- Learn AI-200 topics through production scenarios.

Deliverables:
- Azure AI services
- Semantic Kernel
- Azure AI Search
- RAG implementation
- Prompt orchestration
- AI evaluation

---

## Phase 4 – Engineering Mastery
Week 8

Goal:
- Defend every engineering decision.
- Present a production-ready system.

Deliverables:
- Final architecture review
- Mock interviews
- AI-200 review
- Capstone presentation

# Weekly Structure

Every week answers one engineering question.

Week 1
How do we make a local application cloud-ready?

Week 2
How should production applications manage data?

Week 3
How should systems communicate asynchronously?

Week 4
How should services authenticate and remain secure?

Week 5
How do we operate software in production?

Week 6
How do AI services integrate into modern applications?

Week 7
How do we build trustworthy AI-powered cloud systems?

Week 8
Can I confidently defend every architectural decision?

---

# Daily Learning Framework

Every study day follows the same structure.

## 1. Problem

Start from a realistic business problem.

Never begin by studying a service in isolation.

---

## 2. Design

Discuss possible solutions.

Compare trade-offs.

Write or update an ADR when appropriate.

---

## 3. Build

Implement the solution in Zapas.

No throwaway projects.

---

## 4. Explain

Verbally explain:

- Why this solution?
- Why not alternatives?
- Benefits
- Risks
- Costs
- Operational impact

---

## 5. Defend

Conduct a Tech Lead discussion.

Challenge assumptions.

Explore alternative architectures.

---

## 6. Reflect

Record:

- What I learned
- What changed my mind
- Remaining questions
- Improvements for tomorrow

---

# Friday Tech Lead Review

Every Friday:

- Demonstrate progress.
- Review architecture.
- Review ADRs.
- Explain trade-offs.
- Receive challenges.
- Plan improvements.

---

# Rules of the Residency

- Build before memorizing.
- Simplicity wins until proven otherwise.
- Measure before optimizing.
- Never introduce technology without a business need.
- Every service must have a clear justification.
- Every architectural decision deserves documentation.
- Every feature should improve the repository.
- If you cannot explain it clearly, you do not understand it deeply enough.

# Graduation Criteria

A successful graduate should be able to:

- Design cloud-native systems.
- Explain architectural trade-offs.
- Build production-ready Azure applications.
- Integrate AI services responsibly.
- Operate and troubleshoot cloud systems.
- Communicate like a senior engineer.
- Pass AI-200 with genuine understanding rather than memorization.
